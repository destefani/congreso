## 2. New President and Government

### Inauguration (March 11, 2026)

Jose Antonio Kast (Republican Party) was inaugurated as President of Chile for the 2026-2030 term in a ceremony at the Salon de Honor of the National Congress in Valparaiso. The presidential sash was placed by Senate President Paulina Nunez.

### Inaugural Speech - "Government of Emergency"

Kast delivered his first speech from a balcony of La Moneda, framing his administration as a "government of emergency." Key quotes:

- "We receive a country in worse conditions than we could imagine. A country with weakened public finances. A country where organized crime and drug trafficking have advanced. A country where families feel abandoned by the State."
- "A government of emergency is not a slogan; it is order where there is chaos; it is relief where there is pain; it is a firm hand where there is impunity."
- "We will recover our country, our streets, our institutions, our hope."
- "Chile has real adversaries" who "have taken over our neighborhoods, corrupted our youth, and sown terror in our communities."
- "Those who have entered violating our borders to commit crimes, exploit others, or turn our soil into no man's land" are also "adversaries of Chile."
- El Mostrador described his speech as having a "Portalian tone" (referencing Diego Portales, the 19th-century authoritarian conservative statesman).

### First Six Decrees (signed March 11, 2026)

1. **National Border Closure Policy** - Geographic delimitation of the most vulnerable sectors of the Bolivia border, increased military deployment in the Macrozona Norte
2. **Appointment of Northern Macrozone Commissioner** - Ex-Vice Admiral Alberto Soto named commissioner for Arica, Tarapaca, and Antofagasta regions to execute migration plan
3. **Total State Audit** - Comprehensive audit of all government spending during the Boric period (2022-2026), coordinated by Finance Ministry and Segpres, with three axes: technical, political, and probity
4. **Environmental Permit Unblocking ("Permisologia")** - Resolution of 51 pending environmental impact assessment appeals, representing US$16 billion in stalled investment projects. Deadlines: 90 days for overdue appeals, 30 business days for new appeals to SEA, 60 business days for Ministerial Committee appeals
5. **Regional Reconstruction** - MINVU to lead reconstruction in Valparaiso, Nuble, and Biobio (post-wildfire)
6. **Frontier Shield Plan ("Plan Escudo Fronterizo")** - Army designated to lead northern border protection with four strategic axes and concrete deadlines

## 3. States of Exception

### Macrozona Sur (Araucania / Biobio)

The state of constitutional emergency in La Araucania region and the provinces of Arauco and Biobio (Biobio region) was extended for 30 additional days starting February 26, 2026. The outgoing Boric government described this as its "last requested extension."

Under the state of exception, violence decreased 28% year-over-year in 2025 and 79% compared to 2021.

The new delegate for La Araucania, Francisco Ljubetic, stated that the Kast government is "preparing decrees for the region" and that there are currently "no elements to end this measure." The state of exception is set to expire at the end of March 2026, and Kast is expected to renew it and potentially strengthen it.

Agricultural organizations (e.g., Agricultores de Malleco) are calling for stronger measures and greater military support.

### Northern Border (Macrozona Norte)

The Senate unanimously approved a 90-day extension of the critical infrastructure framework in Arica y Parinacota, Tarapaca, and Antofagasta, allowing Armed Forces to operate in border control and migration enforcement.

Under the Kast government, this has been dramatically escalated with the "Plan Escudo Fronterizo" (see Section 4).

**EROSION ASSESSMENT:** States of exception have been continuously renewed for over 1,000 days under Boric. Kast is expected to maintain and expand them. The normalization of military deployment for civilian policing tasks continues to deepen.

## 4. Security and Migration Policy

### Plan Escudo Fronterizo (Frontier Shield Plan)

The Kast government's most dramatic early action. Signed by decree on March 11, 2026, key elements include:

- **Physical barriers:** Construction of a 3-meter-deep trench and 5-meter walls at the northern border, with fencing, watchtowers, and perimeter barriers
- **Military deployment:** At least 3,000 military personnel permanently stationed for surveillance, territorial control, and elimination of unauthorized crossings. This represents a ~100% increase from previous levels (exceeding 600 in Arica alone)
- **Technology:** Drones, thermal cameras, motion sensors, and biometric identification systems
- **Detention centers:** Creation of internment centers for irregular migrants, focused on Colchane (Bolivia border) and Chacalluta (Peru border)
- **Timeline:** 90-day implementation window
- **Comparison:** Media widely compared the plan to Trump's border wall strategy (CNN, France 24, multiple outlets used "Kast, como Trump" framing)

### Mass Deportation Plan

Kast assumes office with a promise to deport approximately 340,000 migrants in irregular situations (per National Migration Service data as of December 2023, ~75% Venezuelan). Specific measures:

- Converting irregular migration into a crime
- Limiting public benefits (health, education, housing) for undocumented migrants
- Large-scale expulsion plan prioritizing those with criminal records
- Appointment of ex-Vice Admiral Alberto Soto as Northern Macrozone Commissioner specifically to execute the migration plan

### Human Rights Concerns

Human rights organizations have warned these policies could:
- Criminalize migration in violation of international obligations regarding asylum and refuge
- Create a security-only response ignoring humanitarian dimensions
- Violate Chile's commitments under the 1951 Refugee Convention and American Convention on Human Rights

## 5. Constitutional and Institutional Changes

### Supreme Court Crisis - Four Vacancies

The Supreme Court has four vacant positions as of January 2026 due to:
- Retirement of Minister Maria Teresa Letelier Ramirez
- Dismissal of Ministers Angela Vivanco, Sergio Munoz, and Diego Simpertigue

President Kast will appoint these positions (from five-person lists proposed by the Court, with Senate approval), giving him significant influence over the highest court.

### Contraloria General de la Republica

- On February 27, 2026, the Contraloria issued Resolution No. 3 of 2026, updating exemption rules from the 2024 framework, effective June 1, 2026. This streamlines the "toma de razon" review process for state acts.
- On March 10, 2026, the Supreme Court issued a ruling setting limits on the Contraloria's ability to abstain from processing claims, ordering it to continue processing civil servant complaints based on "legitimate trust" principles.

### Tribunal Constitucional

No specific reforms proposed yet by the Kast government regarding the TC. However, given four Supreme Court vacancies and the TC's partially Supreme Court-appointed composition, the new government will have significant appointment power.

### INDH (National Institute of Human Rights)

The Third National Human Rights Plan 2026-2029 (PNDH) was approved by the Boric government's Interministerial Committee before the transition, committing to 175 actions across five strategic objectives. It remains to be seen whether the Kast government will implement, modify, or ignore this plan.

### State Audit as Institutional Tool

Kast's "Total State Audit" decree orders comprehensive fiscal auditing of all government agencies for the 2022-2026 period. While ostensibly a probity measure, critics note it could function as a political weapon against the previous government and its appointees.

**EROSION ASSESSMENT:** The combination of four Supreme Court vacancies to fill, control of the Senate presidency, and the "total audit" mechanism gives the Kast government significant leverage over institutional architecture. The balance depends on Senate negotiations, since judicial appointments require Senate approval.

## 6. Civil Liberties and Human Rights

### Pardons for Police/Military Convicted of Estallido Social Abuses

**This is the single most alarming development for democratic erosion monitoring.**

On March 13, 2026 -- just two days after taking office -- President Kast announced he would use the presidential pardon power to benefit police and military personnel convicted of human rights violations during the 2019 social uprising ("estallido social").

Key facts:
- The Fiscalia records 102 members of the Armed Forces and Police convicted in cases linked to the October 2019 protests
- The 2019 repression left ~40 dead, thousands injured, hundreds with eye injuries from police buckshot, thousands detained, and reports of torture
- Kast justified the pardons by claiming these officers "fulfilled their duty, mandated by the State" during a moment of "extreme violence"
- His Justice Minister Sedini stated decisions would be made "case by case"

**Opposition reaction was fierce:**
- Deputy Andrea Parra (PPD): "I find it provocative that just days into taking office, Kast is announcing pardons for uniformed officers convicted in the social uprising"
- Deputy Emilia Schneider: "If what Kast intends is to free human rights violators it seems abhorrent to me, but it shows his true face"

### Civil Society Organizations' Concerns

Feminist movements, human rights groups, territorial organizations, and unions have issued warnings about:
- Potential rollbacks on the three-grounds abortion law
- Continuity of reproductive rights and gender equity state programs
- Historical memory, truth, and justice policies
- Appointments of individuals who defended Pinochet or held revisionist positions on dictatorship-era human rights violations
- Criminalization of social protest

### UN Human Rights Examination

Chile is scheduled to be examined by the UN Committee on Human Rights, with indigenous rights and the Ley Lafkenche (ECMPO - Espacios Costeros Maritimos para Pueblos Originarios) among the critical points.

**EROSION ASSESSMENT:** The pardons announcement is a direct signal of impunity for state violence. Combined with the "Portalian" rhetoric, the military deployments, and the framing of migrants as "adversaries," this constitutes a significant escalation in authoritarian signaling within the first week of government.

## 8. Congress Dynamics

### Leadership of Both Chambers

**Senate:** Paulina Nunez elected president (39 votes); Ivan Moreira (UDI) as vice president.

**Chamber of Deputies:** Jorge Alessandri (UDI) elected president with 78 votes, defeating Pamela Jiles (PDG) who had 76 votes. This was described as the Kast government's "first legislative victory." Felipe Camano (IND-DC) became first vice president; Ximena Ossandon (RN) second vice president.

The center-right controls the leadership of both chambers, giving the government favorable procedural positioning.

### Legislative Agenda - "Plan Desafio 90"

The Kast government's 90-day legislative plan includes four major bills:

**1. Tax Reduction Bill:**
- Corporate tax from 27% to 23%
- Additional reduction to 20% (average) for firms hiring at-risk informal workers
- SME tax fixed at 12.5%

**2. Property Tax (Contribuciones) Elimination:**
- Gradual elimination starting with citizens 65+
- Applies only to primary owner-occupied residences

**3. Capital Gains Tax Removal:**
- Eliminates capital gains tax on low-liquidity stock sales
- Reinstates full tax system integration

**4. New Investment Statute:**
- Legal and tax security guarantees for domestic and foreign investors
- Targets large investments with technology transfer

### "National Reconstruction" Bill (announced March 14, 2026)

A comprehensive 40-measure package across five axes, announced from the fire-devastated Biobio region:

1. **Physical Reconstruction:** 400 billion pesos for fire response, repatriation regime for capital
2. **Economic Reconstruction:** Temporary VAT elimination on housing (12 months), corporate tax cut, capital gains tax elimination
3. **Institutional Reconstruction:** SEIA reform for faster processing, expedited maritime concessions
4. **Fiscal Reconstruction:** University gratuidad limited to students under 30; halt expansion to new income deciles; moratorium on new universities joining the system; strengthened CAE collection
5. **Legal Order & Security:** Anti-usury protections, increased penalties for cigarette smuggling, enhanced sanctions for illegal migrant transportation

The bill is generating intense debate, with opposition questioning the inclusion of corporate tax cuts and gratuidad restrictions in a "reconstruction" bill.

### Political Dynamics

No single bloc has an absolute majority. The government must negotiate with:
- PDG (14 deputies) as potential swing votes
- Some DC members who may cross over on security issues
- The tied Senate (25-25) requires bipartisan negotiation for any legislation

## 9. Judiciary

### Caso Hermosilla (Caso Audios) - Status

**Criminal proceedings:**
- The Fiscalia Metropolitana Oriente is requesting up to **14 years in prison** for Luis Hermosilla for corruption crimes (bribery, tax crimes, money laundering)
- **18 years** requested for Leonarda Villalobos for the same crimes
- The case is preparing for oral trial, with the preparation hearing scheduled for March 3, 2026
- In January 2026, Hermosilla's defense obtained the right to have prosecutor Lorena Parra testify as a witness
- In February 2026, the Audio-Factop case returned to investigative stage at defense request for reopening
- Precautionary measures reviewed January 30, 2026: Hermosilla and Villalobos maintained in total house arrest; Alvaro Jalaff with nighttime house arrest

**Hermosilla's counter-accusations:**
- Hermosilla accused prosecutor Parra of asking him to "intervene with ministers of the Court" and sending him her curriculum, effectively accusing the lead prosecutor of seeking improper influence

### Caso Muneca Bielorrusa (Belarusian Doll Case) - Angela Vivanco

- **Historic: Former Supreme Court Minister Angela Vivanco placed in preventive detention** on January 30, 2026 -- an unprecedented measure for a former member of Chile's highest court
- Formalized for bribery (cohecho) and money laundering
- Accused of receiving ~11 billion pesos in bribes to influence rulings favorable to the Belarusian consortium Belaz Movitec in litigation against state mining company Codelco
- Her habeas corpus appeal was rejected by the Court of Appeals of San Miguel on March 11, 2026
- Her appeal to the Supreme Court was declared admissible on March 5, 2026

### Broader Judicial Crisis

- Supreme Court President Gloria Ana Chevesich acknowledged "we have crossed a crisis" at the 2026 judicial year inauguration
- Four vacancies in the Supreme Court (Vivanco, Munoz, Simpertigue dismissed; Letelier retired)
- The Power Judicial awarded a contract without competitive bidding ("trato directo") to a company without experience for website renovation, adding to institutional credibility concerns
- Questions raised about judges under investigation (Jean Pierre Matus, Maria Teresa Letelier, Mario Carroza) making decisions about other investigated judges (Antonio Ulloa, Veronica Sabaj)

### Former Fiscal Manuel Guerra

- Ex-fiscal regional Manuel Guerra formalized for aggravated bribery, administrative prevarication, and violation of secrecy, linked to his relationship with Hermosilla
- Defended his innocence on March 10, 2026: "I never sought personal benefit with Luis Hermosilla"

**EROSION ASSESSMENT:** The judicial corruption crisis is the most significant threat to rule of law. The simultaneous prosecution of a former Supreme Court justice, a former regional prosecutor, and Chile's most powerful lawyer, combined with four Supreme Court vacancies that Kast will fill, represents a critical juncture for judicial independence.

## 10. Economic Situation

### GDP Growth

- **2025 actual:** ~2.4% (Banco Central)
- **2026 projection:** 2.0% - 3.0% range (Banco Central IPoM, December 2025); World Bank projects 2.2%
- Capital investment (GFCF) grew above expectations, driven by mining and energy projects: projected +7% (2025), +4.9% (2026), +3.1% (2027)

### Inflation

- Inflation converging to the 3% target
- **February 2026: 2.4%** (below target)
- Expected to reach 3% target during Q1 2026

### Monetary Policy

- Banco Central rate (TPM) at **4.5%** (lowest since January 2022)
- Held steady at January 2026 meeting (unanimous)
- GPM recommends maintaining rate, with cautious stance on further cuts given international risks and proximity to neutral rate

### Employment

- **Unemployment: 8.3%** (trimester Nov 2025 - Jan 2026), up 0.3pp year-over-year
- Female unemployment: 8.7% (down 0.4pp YoY)
- Male unemployment: 8.0% (up 0.8pp YoY)
- Employment growth of 1.2% driven entirely by female employment (+2.7%); zero male employment growth
- **Informal employment: 26.8%** (up 0.5pp YoY)
- Average job search duration exceeds 7 months for women (CIES UDD)

### Pension Reform (Ley 21.735)

The pension reform approved under Boric is being implemented gradually:
- **January 2026:** Beneficio por Anos Cotizados (BAC) payments begin -- 0.1 UF per year contributed, max 25 years
- **February 2026:** Implementation officially begins
- **June 2026:** Single Collection System (SUCC) launches for AFP/IPS joint contribution recovery
- **September 2026:** Protected Return Contribution (CRP) collection begins, including women's compensation for life expectancy differences
- **PGU (Pensión Garantizada Universal):** Expanding from 82+ to 75+ in 2026; reaching all 65+ by 2027
- The reform's impact on GDP for 2026-2030 is being assessed

### Kast's Economic Agenda

The government's economic priorities in the first 90 days focus on:
- Corporate tax reduction (27% to 23%)
- Property tax elimination for seniors
- Capital gains tax removal
- Investment statute for legal certainty
- Environmental permit streamlining (US$16 billion in stalled projects)
- Fiscal austerity: spending cuts of US$6 billion over 18 months (opposed by opposition as dangerous for social programs)

### Wildfires Emergency (January 2026)

Devastating wildfires in Biobio and Nuble regions:
- 34,000+ hectares burned (92% in Biobio)
- 21 deaths, 330+ injuries
- ~22,000 people affected
- 4,100+ homes destroyed
- 400 billion pesos allocated for recovery via the National Reconstruction bill

## 11. International Relations and Geopolitical Context

### China-Chile Submarine Cable Controversy

A major diplomatic incident dominated the February-March transition:
- On January 27, 2026, the Boric government's Transport Ministry approved a concession for a ~20,000km submarine fiber optic cable connecting Chile (Concon, Valparaiso) to Guangdong, China, led by China Mobile, China Unicom, and China Telecom (30-year concession)
- The permit was **annulled 48 hours later** (citing "technical and typographical errors")
- The Trump administration **sanctioned three Boric officials** (including the Transport Minister) by revoking their US visas
- Boric stated Washington made "explicit threats" over the project
- The controversy **broke the presidential transition**: Kast demanded Boric retract public statements about discussions on the cable, accusing Boric of fabricating conversations. Kast effectively ended formal transition cooperation.
- As of March 15, the cable project's future under the Kast government is uncertain, but his alignment with the US position is expected

### Inauguration Diplomacy

14 heads of state and 19 foreign authorities attended the inauguration, potentially the most internationally attended since Chile's return to democracy.

**Notable attendees:**
- Argentine President Javier Milei (key ally; Kast visited Buenos Aires post-election, posed with motosierra)

- Bolivia's President Rodrigo Paz
- US Undersecretary of State Christopher Landau
- Ukraine's Foreign Minister Andrii Sybiha
- Senator Flavio Bolsonaro (son of Brazil's Bolsonaro)

**Notable absence:**
- Brazil's Lula da Silva declined attendance after initially confirming, reportedly due to Kast's invitation of Flavio Bolsonaro

The Kast-Milei bilateral meeting was initially scheduled but then **cancelled** on the day of the inauguration itself.

## Summary: Democratic Erosion Risk Assessment (as of March 15, 2026)

### High-Risk Indicators

1. **Presidential pardons for state violence perpetrators** (announced March 13) -- directly undermines rule of law and accountability for human rights violations
2. **Massive militarization of borders** with 3,000+ troops, trenches, walls, detention centers, and biometric surveillance -- unprecedented in post-Pinochet Chile
3. **"Government of emergency" framing** -- normalizes exceptional governance outside normal democratic channels
4. **Total state audit** targeting the previous government -- potential tool for political persecution
5. **Four Supreme Court vacancies** to be filled by appointment -- transformative judicial power concentration
6. **Nationalist/authoritarian rhetoric** ("adversaries of Chile," Portalian tone, "recover our country")
7. **Only 49% of citizens prefer democracy** over authoritarianism (CEP) -- weak democratic legitimacy baseline

### Mitigating Factors

1. **No congressional supermajority** -- Senate tied 25-25, Chamber requires cross-coalition votes
2. **Active judicial proceedings** against corruption (Hermosilla, Vivanco) -- courts still functioning independently
3. **Active civil society** monitoring government actions
4. **Free press** operating without government interference (so far)
5. **Institutional framework** (Contraloria, TC, INDH) still formally intact
6. **Pension reform implementing** as legislated -- institutional continuity maintained
7. **Central Bank independence** maintained -- TPM decisions based on technical criteria

### Key Indicators to Monitor Next 90 Days

- Will Kast actually issue pardons, and if so, how many?
- Supreme Court appointment nominations
- States of exception: renewal and scope changes
- Immigration enforcement: mass deportation operations
- Environmental permit streamlining: weakening of environmental protections?
- University gratuidad changes: Congressional vote
- Congressional dynamics: will PDG align with government?
- Military/police empowerment measures
- Treatment of social protest
- INDH independence and functioning
- Press freedom incidents
