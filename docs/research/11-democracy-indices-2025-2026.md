# Democracy and Governance Indices for Chile: 2025-2026 Update

*Research compiled: March 15, 2026*

This document compiles the latest democracy and governance indices for Chile, covering data released in 2025 and early 2026. It reflects both the final period of the Boric administration and the election/inauguration of Jose Antonio Kast (December 2025 election, March 11, 2026 inauguration).

---

## 1. V-Dem Democracy Report 2025

**Source:** V-Dem Institute, University of Gothenburg
**Report title:** "25 Years of Autocratization -- Democracy Trumped?"
**Dataset:** V-Dem v15 (covering data through calendar year 2024)
**URL:** https://www.v-dem.net/documents/54/v-dem_dr_2025_lowres_v1.pdf

### Chile's Scores

| Indicator | Score (0-1) |
|-----------|-------------|
| Liberal Democracy Index (LDI) | **0.79** |
| Electoral Democracy Index (EDI) | **0.84** |

### Classification

Chile is classified as a **liberal democracy** -- the highest category in V-Dem's four-tier system (liberal democracy, electoral democracy, electoral autocracy, closed autocracy).

### Regional Context

- Chile is one of only three liberal democracies in Latin America, alongside **Costa Rica** and **Uruguay**.
- Only **4% of Latin American residents** live in liberal democracies.
- 64% of the Latin American population lives in electoral democracies; 6% in electoral autocracies; 3% in closed autocracies.

### Historical Trend

| Year | LDI Score |
|------|-----------|
| 2013 | 0.853 (peak) |
| 2022 | 0.783 |
| 2023 | 0.786 |
| 2024 | 0.79 |
| Historical average (1960-2023) | 0.531 |
| Historical minimum (1974) | 0.033 |

Chile's LDI has been stable in the 0.78-0.79 range since 2022, below its 2013 peak of 0.853.

### Notes

- The V-Dem Dataset v.16 (with 2025 data added) and the Democracy Report 2026 are expected in late March 2026.
- The 2025 data will be critical as it will capture the Kast election period.

## 2. Freedom House -- Freedom in the World 2025

**Source:** Freedom House
**Report covers:** Calendar year 2024
**Published:** February 2025
**URL:** https://freedomhouse.org/country/chile/freedom-world/2025

### Chile's Rating

| Category | Score |
|----------|-------|
| **Status** | **Free** |
| **Aggregate Score** | **95/100** |
| Political Rights | 38/40 |
| Civil Liberties | 57/60 |

### Subcategory Detail

- Most individual indicators score the maximum 4/4.
- Deductions: B4 (3/4), C2 (3/4), F3 (3/4), F4 (3/4), G4 (3/4).

### Year-over-Year Change

- The aggregate score **increased by 1 point** from 94/100 in the previous year.
- **F3 (Protection from illegitimate use of physical force)** improved from 2 to 3 because "the government and law enforcement bodies took additional steps toward security-sector reform and greater accountability for police violence during mass protests in 2019."

### Assessment

Freedom House describes Chile as "a stable democracy that has experienced a significant expansion of political rights and civil liberties since the return of civilian rule in 1990." Ongoing concerns include corruption and unrest linked to land disputes with Indigenous Mapuche people.

## 7. Latinobarometro 2024 -- "La Democracia Resiliente"

**Source:** Corporacion Latinobarometro
**Fieldwork:** August-October 2024, 17 countries
**Published:** December 2024
**URL:** https://www.latinobarometro.org/
**Full report:** https://inep.org/images/2024/TXT/Latinobarometro-Informe_2024.pdf

*Note: The Latinobarometro 2025 report had not been published as of March 2026.*

### Chile-Specific Data

| Indicator | Chile | Regional Average |
|-----------|-------|-----------------|
| **Support for democracy** | **61%** | 52% |
| **Satisfaction with democracy** | **39%** | Below Chile |
| **Life satisfaction** | **64%** | Higher range |
| **Fear of crime** | **77%** | -- |
| **Economic optimism** | **30%** | Lowest in region |

### Key Findings for Chile

- **Support for democracy (61%)**: One of the highest levels in the past 20 years for Chile; above the regional average of 52%.
- **Satisfaction with democracy (39%)**: Significant gap between supporting democracy in principle (61%) and being satisfied with how it functions (39%).
- **Economic pessimism**: Chile has the **lowest economic optimism in the region** at only 30%.
- **Fear of crime (77%)**: Very high, despite Chile having relatively low homicide rates by regional standards.
- **Institutional trust**: Chileans believe political parties (42%), Congress (39%), and the opposition (37%) are "not necessary," leaving what the report calls "an air of authoritarianism."

### Characterization

Latinobarometro director Marta Lagos stated: "Chileans are today the most frightened of the region." Chile is described as "one of the most pessimistic countries on the continent" -- the population perceives democracy as functioning poorly and the country as "captured by economic elites."

## 10. Academic Papers and Reports on Democratic Erosion in Chile (2025-2026)

### 10.1 "Walking towards Democratic Backsliding? Chile at a Crossroads"

**Author:** Javiera Arce Riffo (PhD student, UCL Institute of the Americas; CEGEP, Universidad de Valparaiso)
**Published:** January 19, 2026
**URL:** https://blogs.ucl.ac.uk/americas-blog/2026/01/19/walking-towards-democratic-backsliding-chile-at-a-crossroads/

**Key arguments:**
- Kast's election reflects both global far-right trends and Chile-specific institutional failures
- Democratic erosion is likely to be **selective rather than abrupt**, targeting judicial independence while circumventing Congress via decree laws
- Kast signaled intent to govern by decree: "Congress is not as relevant as the people might imagine"
- Risks to press freedom, gender and minority rights, university autonomy
- Contributing domestic failures: Boric's ineffective governance, two failed constitutional replacements (2022, 2023), fragmented Congress

### 10.2 "Chile's Democracy Faces Its Gravest Test Since Pinochet"

**Authors:** Eduardo Engel and Benjamin Garcia
**Published:** December 8, 2025
**Source:** Social Europe
**URL:** https://www.socialeurope.eu/chiles-democracy-faces-its-gravest-test-since-pinochet

**Key arguments:**
- Chile's institutional achievements (poverty reduced from 59% to 6%, life expectancy matching developed nations, low debt at 42.8% of GDP vs. 110.5% OECD average) are paradoxically under threat
- Citizens' trust in the state has plummeted despite institutional successes
- Political gridlock prevents anti-corruption reforms (beneficial ownership registries, judicial appointment transparency)
- Kast's campaign targets "parasites" in government, threatening merit-based civil service
- Homicides rose 50% (2016-2022), fueling populist "law-and-order" narratives

### 10.3 "From Estallido to Far-Right Restoration: How Chile's Democratic Moment Turned Authoritarian"

**Author:** Luciano Santander Hoces
**Published:** January 15, 2026
**Source:** International Research Group on Authoritarianism and Counter-Strategies (IRGAC)
**URL:** https://irgac.org/articles/from-estallido-to-far-right-restoration-how-chile-s-democratic-moment-turned-authoritarian/

**Key arguments:**
- Kast "rearticulated the authoritarian legacy of the Chilean right" through gremialismo (Catholic conservative doctrine emphasizing depoliticization and state subsidiarity)
- Public approval of the 2019 Estallido dropped from 61.7% (2020) to 28.8% (2022)
- Constitutional rejection rates: 62% (September 2022), 55.78% (December 2023)
- Compulsory voting (introduced 2025) added approximately 5 million voters, benefiting the right
- Kast's project "operates within [democracy's] margins to stifle the very possibilities that democracy allows" -- not overthrowing institutions but rendering progressive change "impossible to conceive"

### 10.4 "Jose Antonio Kast Takes Office: Pragmatist or Radical Right?"

**Source:** Americas Quarterly
**Published:** March 2026
**URL:** https://americasquarterly.org/article/jose-antonio-kast-presidency-chile/

**Key arguments:**
- **Arguments for pragmatism:** Kast lacks charisma to co-opt moderate-right parties; his 16-year legislative career suggests he understands compromise; during international visits he distanced himself from Milei's radicalism and El Salvador's extreme crime policies; supports free trade (traditional conservative, not populist)
- **Arguments for radicalism:** Eight years building a far-right party; campaign promises of massive deportations, border militarization, punitive crime policies; historical misreading of mandates; "unwavering support of the Pinochet dictatorship"
- **Constraints:** No absolute majority in Congress; Senate evenly divided; balance of power in lower house rests with the populist Party of the People

### 10.5 "Latin America 2025: Protest Voting Amid Fragmentation and Democratic Erosion"

**Source:** Latinoamerica21
**URL:** https://latinoamerica21.com/en/latin-america-2025-protest-voting-amid-fragmentation-and-democratic-erosion/

**Chile-specific findings:**
- Chile demonstrated exceptional electoral integrity: results announced "two hours after polls closed and immediately accepted"
- Kast won with 58% of the vote on a platform of "drastic public spending cuts, tough 'law and order' policies, opposition to abortion and marriage equality"
- Chile exemplified the 2025 regional pattern of protest voting -- rejecting incumbents across ideological lines

## Key Observations and Risks

### Positive Indicators
1. Chile retains its **V-Dem liberal democracy** classification -- one of only three in Latin America.
2. Freedom House score **improved to 95/100** with security-sector reform gains.
3. Electoral integrity remains strong: November 2025 elections had **85.3% turnout** and results accepted immediately.
4. IDEA ranks Chile **5th globally in Representation** (0.875).
5. Support for democracy at **61%** is well above the regional average.

### Warning Signs
1. **EIU classification stuck at "flawed democracy"** since 2023, driven by low Political Culture score (5.63).
2. **Press freedom dropped sharply** -- down 17 positions to 69th; Economic indicator particularly weak (88th, 41.94).
3. **CPI stagnant at 63** -- classified as a "Decliner" since losing 10 points from 2014 peak.
4. **Massive gap between democratic support (61%) and satisfaction (39%)** in Latinobarometro.
5. Chile has the **lowest economic optimism in Latin America** (30%) and highest fear of crime (77%).
6. **Institutional trust crisis**: 42% say parties are not necessary; 39% say Congress is not necessary.

### Emerging Risks Under Kast Administration (March 2026 onwards)
1. **Governance by decree**: Kast has signaled intent to bypass Congress; cabinet of business leaders with little political experience.
2. **Breakdown of transition norms**: Withdrawal from power-transition talks with Boric.
3. **Judicial independence**: Selective erosion of counterweights identified as primary risk by multiple analysts.
4. **Press freedom**: Already declining before Kast took office; further threats anticipated.
5. **Indigenous rights**: Heightened risk of Mapuche repression under "law and order" framework.
6. **Science and education**: Kast has hinted at shrinking or eliminating the science ministry.
7. **Congressional constraints as safeguard**: No absolute majority; Senate evenly divided; dependent on coalition-building.
